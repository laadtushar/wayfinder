Wayfinder

Building an explorable-worlds game on Samsung Galaxy XR. The headset, engine, store, and even on-device photoreal capture already ship. The frontier is the world-generation pipeline and the AI in the world.
Frontier ideation · July 2026

Where this sits #▼

The "what's actually new and useful, validated across sources" document for using SLAM3R-style reconstruction plus Unity/Unreal to build a Star-Trek-flavoured planet-exploration game on Galaxy XR. Split into four tiers by adoption risk. Built from ~40 web searches across seven angles, then an adversarial fact-check over 43 load-bearing claims: 35 confirmed, 8 corrected, 0 false.

Three reframes that change what you build

1 · SLAM3R does not run on the headset, and neither does anything in its family. Every feed-forward reconstruction model (SLAM3R, DUSt3R, MASt3R, VGGT, Fast3R) needs a desktop or datacenter GPU with 12–80 GB of memory. The Galaxy XR runs a mobile chip on a few watts. So SLAM3R is an offline authoring tool: scan on your PC, reconstruct on a desktop/cloud GPU, bake a finished world into the app, and the headset only renders pre-built worlds.

2 · The platform is the easy part. Galaxy XR is buyable hardware. Unity 6's Android XR support went general availability on Oct 23 2025. On-device Gaussian-splat rendering already ships on this chip class (Into the Scaniverse). And BRINK Traveler plus Google Maps Immersive View already do "walk real scanned places in XR" natively on Galaxy XR. Your edge must be gameplay, procedural planets, and something nobody else has.

3 · Two of your three names are legally unsafe. "Strange New Worlds" is an active Paramount series title. "Wayfinder" is a live US game trademark (Airship Syndicate) in exactly the game-software classes. Keep "Wayfinder" as an internal codename only.

Tier 1: Use-today #▼

Use today The shippable spine. A solo dev can build a vertical slice from Tier 1 alone.

1.1Unity 6 (GA) as the engine — Godot as the free alternative

GA Oct 23 2025Unity / Godot MITlaunch titles shipped on it

Unity 6's Android XR build target is generally available — ignore anyone saying "still beta" (only the newest 6.5 polish package is). One OpenXR build also runs on XREAL AURA glasses and, with a rebuild, Meta Quest. The free alternative is Godot 4.6.2+ with games already live on Google Play.

Why it matters here: a walkable-planet game needs a mature asset pipeline, the splat/point-cloud tooling in 1.3, foveation, and SpaceWarp — Unity has all of it and the launch games prove the path. Unreal is a trap: its Android XR support is developer-preview only, and Nanite/Lumen aren't usable on a mobile Snapdragon anyway.

The catch: you do not need PolySpatial for a fully-immersive game (it's for apps that float in the home environment, and its visionOS mode needs a paid Unity licence). Skip it — plain OpenXR + XRI is simpler and free.

1.2The offline reconstruction → clean-mesh pipeline (and the licence minefield)

SLAM3R 20+ fps on RTX 4090most weights non-commercialOpen3D = MIT

This is where SLAM3R lives: capture a real place, reconstruct on a desktop/cloud GPU (SLAM3R gives 20+ fps dense point clouds on a 4090), mesh it, decimate and texture, import the static asset into Unity. The net is only step zero — the headset renders a decimated mesh, not a 20-million-point cloud.

Why the licence detail is load-bearing: SLAM3R is built on DUSt3R, so its weights inherit a non-commercial licence — as do MASt3R, MASt3R-SLAM, Fast3R. Prototype freely; you cannot ship a paid game on them. The commercially-clean set: VGGT-1B-Commercial, MoGe / MoGe-2 (MIT), and Pi3's code (BSD).

capture -> SLAM3R/VGGT (desktop/cloud CUDA GPU) -> point cloud
  -> Open3D Screened Poisson  (MIT, commercially clean)
       pcd.estimate_normals(); create_from_point_cloud_poisson()
  -> decimate ~100k-500k tris (Blender) + xatlas UV + bake texture
  -> glTF/FBX -> Unity

The catch: use Open3D, not MeshLab, in the shipped pipeline — MeshLab is GPLv3 (copyleft) and drags obligations into your product. NVIDIA NKSR gives more detail but ships under a restrictive research licence.

1.3On-device Gaussian splatting as the runtime look — the "diorama per scene" pattern

aras-p MITstandalone Quest-provenScaniverse free

A decimated mesh looks worse than reality. 3D Gaussian splatting (store the scene as millions of tiny coloured blobs, render by sorting and blending) both looks better and runs on the mobile chip. Proof on your exact hardware class: Into the Scaniverse renders user splats on-device on Quest, no cloud.

Why it matters here: your renderer is aras-p's UnityGaussianSplatting (MIT, VR-tested standalone on Quest 3, needs Vulkan/D3D12). Copy the proven pattern: one bounded splat scene at a time, swapped as you travel — a diorama per location, respecting the ~200k–400k-splats-per-view budget.

The catch that shapes gameplay: splats have no collision, don't receive engine lighting, and their lighting is baked in from capture time. Every splat world needs an invisible polygon collision mesh under it, and anything the player touches (creatures, doors, your AI companion) must be a normal mesh actor. KIRI's Mesh-Inclusive 3DGS auto-generates that collision mesh. Plan for splat backdrop + mesh actors from day one.

1.4Procedural planets, the classic way — the part you fully own

free / openno licence costthe "new planets" pillar

Generative AI does not give you a whole planet. Start from Sebastian Lague's Procedural Planets: mesh six terrain faces onto a sphere (the cube-sphere technique real planet games use), stack noise for terrain, shade by elevation. Layer on the No Man's Sky recipe: multi-frequency noise (fractal Brownian motion) + domain warping + seed-driven biomes.

Why it matters here: this is near-infinite varied planets from math without storing them — the heart of replayability, and the part of Wayfinder you own outright rather than rent.

The catch: it's a technique corpus, not importable code, and VR adds the constraint that terrain chunks must generate inside the frame and thermal budget. For hand-crafted hero regions, Gaea 2 gives film-grade erosion — but it's regional heightfield, not a whole globe.

1.5Publishing to Google Play — the concrete path and its gates

$25 one-time~15% cut on first $1M12-tester / 14-day gate

Pay the one-time $25 fee, build an Android App Bundle, publish to the dedicated Android XR release track. One manifest line marks it a fully-immersive XR app. Pass the quality guidelines (the constraints table).

Two corrections in your favour: Android XR is exempt from the Aug 31 2026 "target API 36" deadline (with Wear/Auto/TV). And the June 2026 billing change is ~15% effective on your first $1M — better than Meta's/Apple's 30%.

1.6The comparables that de-risk the plan — and the IP green light

BRINK + Google Maps ship on Galaxy XRAbdin v. CBS = green light

Two shipping apps prove your loop is buildable here: BRINK Traveler (58 photogrammetry environments you walk inside) and Google Maps Immersive View (Unity-powered, ask Gemini about a landmark). Warning from the graveyard: Google Earth VR was abandoned because sightseeing without a game loop doesn't sustain.

The IP green light: Abdin v. CBS affirmed non-infringement over a space-exploration game — the generic idea "explore strange new worlds" is free. Copy none of the specific expression: no delta insignia, no Enterprise, no warp/phaser/LCARS, no Klingon/Vulcan.

The catch: Paramount enforces hard even against free fan work — the Stage 9 fan VR Enterprise was killed by cease-and-desist, the closest analog to what you're building. Sell the feeling of discovery; never borrow the furniture. Run a trademark clearance in classes 9 + 41 before any public name. (Not legal advice.)

Tier 2: Use-soon #▼

Use soon Research-mature, build with care. Each carries an early-adopter tax.

2.1Generative world models with exportable assets — Marble and HY-World 2.0

Marble public Nov 12 2025Pro $35/mo = commercialHY-World 2.0 Apache 2.0

The key split is exportable-asset vs closed-stream. World Labs' Marble (Fei-Fei Li's company) turns text/photo/video into a persistent 3D world and exports meshes and splats (Pro tier for commercial rights). The open counterpart is Tencent HY-World 2.0 (Apache 2.0), exporting mesh + splats + point cloud + depth + camera params, self-hostable.

Why it matters here: turn a concept image into an explorable region or a hero landmark. Choose mesh for on-device performance, splats only for fidelity backdrops.

The catch: worlds are room/region scale, not planets; Marble's credit model costs real money to iterate; auto-generated topology needs cleanup.

2.2Single-object generative 3D — the prop / creature / ship workhorse

TRELLIS 2 Dec 2025, MITTripo / Rodin / MeshyGLB → Unity

Microsoft TRELLIS 2 (4B params, MIT) turns one photo into a game-ready GLB in seconds via ComfyUI — free and unrestricted. Hosted options: Tripo (fastest, includes rigging for creatures), Rodin/Meshy (cleaner topology), all export GLB.

Why it matters here: populate planets fast — rocks, plants, artifacts, your original (non-Enterprise) ships, animated creatures via Tripo's rigging.

The catch: one object per generation, not a scene; auto topology needs a decimation/LOD pass for the mobile budget.

2.3The reconstruction frontier — VGGT, Fast3R, MASt3R-SLAM

VGGT CVPR 2025 Best Papermost weights non-commercialVGGT-1B-Commercial exists

VGGT (Meta+Oxford) predicts poses, depth, dense point maps in one pass (1.2B params, a scene in under a second) and is the one top model with a commercial checkpoint. Fast3R does 1,500 images in one pass (non-commercial). MASt3R-SLAM gives poses + geometry from a moving camera (~15 fps on a 4090, non-commercial).

Why it matters here: VGGT-1B-Commercial is the shippable choice for turning a photo set of a real place into geometry; Fast3R for whole-location photo sweeps.

The catch: VRAM ceilings mean long captures must be tiled; point maps are noisy at depth edges; only VGGT-1B-Commercial is legally shippable.

2.4The mobile-GPU performance levers — SpaceWarp and foveation

Unity + Android XR Extensionseye-tracked foveation

Application SpaceWarp (render at half rate, synthesise the in-between frames) is the biggest single lever for heavy scenes. Dynamic foveated rendering (full detail only where the eye looks) is the second, and how VRSplat claws back frame budget.

Why it matters here: these are what make dense reconstructed worlds hold 72–90 fps on the mobile chip.

The catch: SpaceWarp isn't free quality — it needs correct motion vectors and depth, and dense geometry with bad motion vectors shows artifacts. Validate on the real device, not the emulator.

Tier 3: Bet-on #▼

Bet on A 12-month moat if you build it. Almost nobody is doing these in a shipping game yet.

3.1A Gemini-powered in-world companion — your "ship's computer"

Gemini Live APIthe single biggest differentiator

Gemini is integrated at the OS level of Android XR and reachable via the Gemini Live API — real-time voice + vision. No prior VR platform had first-party multimodal AI in the system. For Wayfinder: a companion that literally sees what the player sees and reacts — a science officer that narrates the planet, answers "what is that structure?", logs discoveries.

Why it matters here: this is what turns "another scanned-world walker" into "the exploration game with a mind in it." Google Maps ships the primitive version; nobody's built it as a game companion.

The catch: the clean path for calling Gemini Live from inside a Unity/Unreal immersive game isn't documented yet (previewed integrations are with ARCore/glasses). You'll pioneer it as a streaming client, and design around latency, per-call cost, and camera-to-cloud privacy. Bet on it anyway.

3.2Breaking the diorama ceiling — mesh-bound and LOD splats

NanoGS ~Mar 2026Voyager 6.6× speedupSqueezeMe on Quest 3

The Tier 1 pattern caps you at one room per scene. NanoGS brings Nanite-style level-of-detail to splats. Voyager does city-scale mobile LOD (6.6× speedup, 85% energy savings). Mesh-bound methods (GS-Verse, MeshSplats, KIRI) bind Gaussians to a mesh so physics and collision work. SqueezeMe already runs three splat avatars on-device on Quest 3.

Why it matters here: these map the path from "photo of a room" to "walkable district," and let a splat creature deform or be collided with.

The catch: mostly papers and brand-new plugins, no proven Galaxy XR numbers — but building tooling around them now is a real lead.

3.3Cloud-streamed splats — the escape hatch from the on-device budget

Meta Hyperscape Sept 2025server cost + always-online

Meta Hyperscape / Project Avalanche renders whole photoreal splat rooms in the cloud and streams them to the headset as a thin client — quality no standalone GPU can match, because it isn't rendered locally.

Why it matters here: it delivers worlds beyond the ~400k-splat on-device ceiling. The bet: it's Meta's closed system, so for you this is a "build your own splat-streaming backend" bet, or wait for a service.

The catch: real server cost, network latency, always-online. A solo dev can't trivially replicate it — but it proves the on-device budget isn't the final word.

3.4Next-gen reconstruction — Pi3 and MapAnything

Pi3 ICLR 2026Pi3 code = BSDMapAnything Sept 2025

Pi3 drops the fixed-reference-frame dependence of DUSt3R/VGGT for smoother, more robust output — and it's the first top model whose code is BSD-3 (commercial-friendly) even though weights are non-commercial. MapAnything is a universal metric model with swappable backends.

Why it matters here: for a longer build, higher-quality, more stable reconstructions, with pipeline code you can legally use.

The catch: both are new with rougher tooling than VGGT; Pi3 weights still need commercial licensing or your own training.

Tier 4: Speculative #▼

Speculative Probably 18+ months out. Track it, don't build on it.

4.1Real-time interactive world models (Genie 3 / Project Genie)

Project Genie Jan 29 2026no export, ~1 min memory

Genie 3 generates a navigable photorealistic world frame-by-frame as you move; Project Genie launched to US AI Ultra subscribers.

Why not yet: 720p at 24 fps, ~1 minute of world consistency then it forgets, no mesh/splat export, cloud-only. A dead end as build material today — but the day it becomes exportable and persistent, "infinite explorable planets" changes shape. Track it.

4.2On-device neural reconstruction

QuantVGGT, research-only

QuantVGGT (4-bit VGGT, ~3.7× memory reduction) is the first step toward reconstruction on smaller hardware — but still targets desktop GPUs, research-only.

Why not yet: the VRAM/power gap to a mobile chip is orders of magnitude. Worth tracking because the day it works, a player could scan their own room into the game live — a killer feature.

4.3Interactive-video world models (Odyssey, Decart Oasis)

closed stream, per-second pricing

Odyssey-2 and Decart Oasis stream controllable worlds one frame at a time. Same closed-stream limits as Genie 3, and Oasis is scoped to driving simulation.

Why not yet: no exportable geometry, cloud-only, wrong shape for a standalone game. Track lightly.

4.4Real-astronomical-data planets

Elite Dangerous Stellar Forge

A frontier content bet: Elite Dangerous seeds ~160,000 systems from real star catalogues. Real exoplanet and terrain datasets give authenticity procedural noise can't.

Why worth knowing: "these are real worlds" is a differentiator no purely-procedural competitor can claim, and the datasets are public — at the cost of building a data pipeline.

Build first #▼

If I had one engineer and one month:

Ship a vertical slice of one bounded, hand-picked scanned real place — rendered as an on-device Gaussian splat with a collision mesh under it, in Unity 6 (GA), with a Gemini Live companion that reacts to what you look at — sideloaded onto the Galaxy XR.

That one slice proves the entire spine end to end with only shippable-today pieces. It replaces three assumptions:

• that you need SLAM3R at runtime (you don't — it's offline authoring, and the slice can start from a Scaniverse capture with no reconstruction at all);
• that you need Unreal for fidelity (you don't — splats out-look decimated meshes on this chip and run standalone);
• that a scanned-world walker is enough (it isn't — Google Earth VR died proving that; the Gemini companion is the game).

Procedural planets, your own reconstruction pipeline, and generative worlds are refinements you layer after the loop is proven. The splat-diorama-plus-AI-companion slice tells you in four weeks whether Wayfinder is a game or a tech demo — and if it lands, no other Galaxy XR exploration app currently has it.

Not optional: pick a different public name before building any brand. "Wayfinder" and "Strange New Worlds" are both taken in ways that will cost you. The exploration feeling is yours; those two words aren't.
